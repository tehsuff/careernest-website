Empowering Individuals to Create Vibrant Inclusive Communities

WorkVentures was founded way back in 1979 as Peninsula Community Services. Since then, we have undergone many challenges, changes and developments, however, our core objective remains the same: Empowering Individuals to Create Vibrant Inclusive Communities.

For those of us lucky enough to be digitally independent, it can be difficult to imagine surviving even one day without the convenience technology affords us. But sadly, not everyone in this country has the same financial means, relevant skills or access to digital technology to be able to feel digitally included.

We believe creating access to technology for all and mastering computer literacy within our communities is key to establishing an individual's independence and creating a more socially inclusive community.

Discover how we empower individuals all over Australia

Through our partnerships with other like-minded NFPs and corporates, we push the boundaries to:

  • Breathe new life into technology with our ability to recycle technology. By refurbishing and selling donated computers, we make technology accessible to those that would otherwise be digitally excluded.
  • Empower individuals by providing alternative employment pathways and assisting in the development of skills for those that would otherwise be left behind.
  • Provide recycled technology and other solutions to assist with reducing the environmental footprint of the organisations we work with.

All profits from our social enterprise are reinvested into our digital inclusion strategies and programs for young people, which aim to empower individuals and create inclusive communities in Australia.

Our Mission

WorkVentures is Australia's leading IT social enterprise. We are committed to creating inclusive, vibrant communities. We help young people gain meaningful skills, training, and jobs and provide affordable technology and connectivity to those who are digitally excluded.

Social Inclusion for Young People

We know that having twenty-first century skills and relevant work experience are the key to ongoing full-time employment for young people.

We support young people by providing work experience, traineeships and jobs within our social enterprise and with other like-minded not-for-profit, government and corporate partners.

By providing young people with opportunities to gain tangible skills and become job-ready in a safe and nurturing environment, we encourage them to ignite their potential and, as a result, help create vibrant, inclusive communities.

Reducing the Digital Divide

The digital divide in Australia is deepening. The key to closing the gap and creating more socially inclusive communities is creating access to affordable, internet connected technology, and enabling people with the skills to use it.

We provide affordable, reliable computers to individuals and non-profits who otherwise could not access or afford this technology. Computers are donated by our partners and restored to an as-new condition, giving new life to technology and creating environmentally-sustainable IT solutions.

Meaningful Employment Opportunities

At WorkVentures, we are passionate about igniting the potential in young Australians. Our Youth Employment Solutions team work hard to ensure no young person is left behind.

During our traineeship program, trainees complete a practical qualification while working full-time in paid employment and gaining real-world work experience. We also provide work placement opportunities for high school students and offer cyber career pathway programs.

Our mission is to reduce youth unemployment by empowering young people with meaningful skills and jobs.

Youth Unemployment in Australia

Youth unemployment in Australia is twice that of the rest of the population. As a society, it is important that we tackle the crucial issue of how to reduce unemployment across the country.

The main reasons young people struggle to find employment include lack of experience, insufficient qualifications, limited networks, and barriers related to social disadvantage.

Our Solution: Combating Youth Unemployment

No matter what industry you are in, hiring a trainee is a great option for recruiting junior staff to fulfil your entry-level business requirements and support a young person in achieving a meaningful career.

We source trainees and place them with your organisation. Together we provide a foundational career pathway that suits the trainee and your business. Trainees are a cost-effective staffing solution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is WorkVentures?
WorkVentures is a not-for-profit social enterprise. WorkVentures is also an accredited Group Training Organisation (GTO). Our organisation focuses on maximising support for young people and helping other organisations do the same.
What is a traineeship?
A traineeship is an entry-level position providing both paid work experience and a relevant qualification to the trainee.
What is the benefit of a traineeship to the trainee?
Young people are more likely to find sustained, long-term employment if they have one year of paid work experience related to their field of study. In a traineeship, they are offered both a qualification and experience simultaneously.
What are the benefits of a WorkVentures traineeship to a trainee?
WorkVentures offers a tried and tested support model that means you are 40% more likely to complete your traineeship than the national average.
What are the benefits for a host company?
Traineeships are a cost-effective way to fill an entry-level position. They help build talent for the future of the business. WorkVentures makes it easy for host employers to help young people find employment.
What types of roles is a traineeship suitable for?
Our trainees are employed in a range of roles. These include office administration, legal support, customer service, IT support and helpdesk, HR, community services, and financial services support. If you have an entry-level position you are looking to fill, please reach out, and we will be able to advise if we are able to assist.
How long do traineeships last?
Traineeships are typically 12-24 months, depending on the qualification. IT traineeships typically last 24 months, others are generally between 12-18 months.
Do hosts employ the trainees after the traineeship is complete?
While there is no obligation for ongoing employment, we find that many employers do keep on their trainees for further employment. On average, 88% of trainees are offered ongoing employment with their host employer. WorkVentures works with the host employer on a managed exit plan and provides individual support to the trainee for the next stage of their career and education.
What is the qualification that a trainee obtains?
The trainee will complete a Certificate III or IV in one of a number of disciplines during their traineeship. The right course is selected to match the role that is offered.
Who manages the traineeship coursework?
WorkVentures' service includes coordinating the traineeship coursework, submission, and completion. We work with Australian Government agencies and Registered Training Organisations (RTOs) to ensure qualification requirements are met. RTOs deliver nationally recognised training in the VET sector.
Does the trainee attend TAFE for their studies?
No, coursework is completed online. Host employers are required to allow trainees three hours per week to complete their coursework. Host employers must provide an appropriate space for the studies to be completed.
Do trainees sit exams?
No, the assessment is competency-based. In order to pass each unit, the trainee must be able to demonstrate that they have developed relevant skills and knowledge.

At WorkVentures, we are passionate about bridging the digital divide. We strive to ensure that all Australians have equal opportunities to access technology. We support individuals and organisations around the country by providing affordable devices, connectivity and technical support.

In our IT Solutions department, we refurbish pre-owned corporate devices and sell them at affordable prices. We also provide six months of warranty and ongoing technical support.

Australians Face Digital Exclusion

With the world becoming increasingly reliant on technology, it is of great importance that all people and organisations are able to access the digital world.

1 in 4 Australians are digitally excluded, which means that they do not have the same opportunities as those with regular access to technology.

Organisations that lack digital literacy fall behind. In 2021, 47% of not-for-profits were not satisfied with the way they used technology.

Our Solution: Bridging the Digital Divide

At WorkVentures, we believe every individual and organisation deserves the chance to have access to the digital world, IT skills, and necessary support to achieve their goals.

We collaborate with our corporate partners to enable individuals and not-for-profits to gain access to affordable, reliable IT solutions and ongoing tech support.

Our History

Take a stroll through our history and find out how we've developed over the years.

Home› About Us› Our History
1979

Founded as Peninsula Community Services

WorkVentures was founded in the Malabar/La Perouse area in Sydney's south-east. The organisation initially ran a range of programs including holiday programs, a recycling venture, and other community-based projects.

1984

Focus Shifts to Employment & Training

From 1984 onwards the focus shifted to employment, training, and technical services — the core of what WorkVentures continues to do today.

2002

Co-founder of Social Ventures Australia

WorkVentures was one of the four organisations that established Social Ventures Australia, a landmark moment in Australian social enterprise history.

2005

Prime Minister's Award for Excellence

WorkVentures and Microsoft Australia won the Prime Minister's Award for Excellence in Community Business Partnership, Longevity category for their 19-year partnership.

2008

Traineeship Program Launched

WorkVentures launched its dedicated traineeship program, focused on inclusion and serving all young Australians — including First Nations youth, people with disabilities, and other underrepresented groups.

2010

Founder Honoured with Order of Australia

Former CEO and Founder Steve Lawrence was appointed an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO) in the 2010 Australia Day Honours list for his service to the community through the development and implementation of not-for-profit ventures to create social change.

2017

Partnership with Uniting

WorkVentures partnered with Uniting on First Nations traineeship programs and diversity programs, helping people develop their careers and gain practical skills while working toward a qualification.

2023

HLB Mann Judd Appointed as Auditors

Following the resignation of BDO Audit Pty Ltd, HLB Mann Judd Assurance (NSW) Pty Ltd were appointed as auditors, demonstrating WorkVentures' commitment to strong corporate governance.

WorkVentures Circular IT (ITAD)

WorkVentures is the only ITAD in Australia whose profits are 100% dedicated to social impact.

At WorkVentures, we believe IT asset disposition (ITAD) should do more than clear out old equipment — it should create measurable value, protect your data, and contribute to a more sustainable and inclusive future. Through WorkVentures CircularIT, we transform end-of-life technology into opportunity: securing sensitive information, reducing e-waste, recovering financial value, and delivering tangible social impact.

Data Security

Protecting sensitive information is critical. WorkVentures CircularIT services ensure that all data-bearing devices are wiped or destroyed to the highest industry standards, safeguarding your business, your customers, and your reputation. Every process is fully documented with certificates of data destruction and audit-ready reporting.

Hassle-Free ITAD

Managing used equipment can drain valuable IT resources. As your trusted for-purpose ITAD partner, WorkVentures CircularIT handles everything, from logistics and asset tracking to compliance and reporting, so your team can focus on what matters most: running your business.

Social Impact

Organisations seeking to do good choose ITAD programs that do more than protect data and the environment: they create positive social impact and contribute to your ESG outcomes. Donated or refurbished devices can empower communities, support digital inclusion, and strengthen your organisation's social responsibility profile.

How We Create Impact

Redirect residual value by donating devices to trusted NFPs and inclusion partners. As a co-founder of the National Device Bank, WorkVentures ensures every donation is backed by rigorous impact and sustainability reporting, so you can see the difference your contribution makes for both communities and the environment.

National Device Bank

Bridging the Digital Divide One Device at a Time

Home› IT Solutions› National Device Bank

National Device Bank

The National Device Bank is a co-founded initiative between WorkVentures, Good360 Australia, and Good Things Australia. Together, we combine decades of expertise to drive digital inclusion and support communities.

WorkVentures has delivered over 100,000 devices to underserved Australians. Good360 connects donors with over 4,500 charities to support those in need.

How It Works

  • One device commercially remarketed to fund the program
  • One device refurbished and donated via the National Device Bank
  • Windows image applied with core base software requirements
  • Recipient/charity identified, and device delivered with supporting digital support documents
  • Ongoing support, digital literacy education, and connectivity provided to recipients

WorkVentures can provide certified data erasure and device sanitisation services in the absence of an existing arrangement.

The Digital Divide

The ramifications of digital exclusion are far-reaching. The National Device Bank addresses this challenge by ensuring every donated device creates real, measurable change in the lives of Australians who need it most.

Donate Technology

Your old devices can change lives. When you donate technology to WorkVentures, you are helping to bridge the digital divide and create more inclusive communities across Australia.

How Your Donation Helps

Donated devices are professionally refurbished by our technical team and then distributed to individuals, families, and not-for-profit organisations who cannot afford technology. Every device is fully tested and comes with technical support.

Since 2000, WorkVentures has distributed over 100,000 technology packs to underserved Australians — and it's all made possible by generous technology donations from our corporate and individual partners.

Donate Today

What We Accept

  • Laptops and desktop computers (less than 8 years old)
  • Tablets and iPads
  • Monitors
  • Printers and accessories
  • Mobile phones
  • Networking equipment

All donated devices undergo secure data erasure to protect your organisation's information before being refurbished and redistributed.
